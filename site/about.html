Built by someone who's shipped before.

GitGate is a small open-source project. No hosted product, no signup, no account. Just a single CLI on npm and the YAML you already have.

GitGate is built by George Rios, founder of flarefound — a Cloudflare-focused development consultancy. 25+ years of shipping production software: enterprise architecture, distributed systems, high-scale platforms. The kind of experience that comes from shipping across multiple decades of technology shifts — on-prem to cloud, monoliths to microservices, cloud to edge.

flarefound does one thing: builds software on Cloudflare. Not AWS, not Azure, not multi-cloud. Workers, D1, R2, Durable Objects, Containers, Queues, Workers AI — that's the entire stack. Companies come to flarefound when they want to build new products on Cloudflare's edge or migrate existing workloads off traditional infrastructure.

GitGate started as a question: your CI loop is `git push → wait → fail → repeat`. What if it ran on every save instead? That answer turned into a runner that reads your .github/workflows/*.yml, executes it locally, and finishes 5–9× faster than act on standard workflows — 30× on services, where act doesn't actually complete. Same YAML, no new config.

The runner CLI, the @gitgate/ci TypeScript pipeline SDK, the @gitgate/git-core pure-TypeScript git engine, and the @gitgate/cli companion tool are all Apache 2.0 on npm. The generated YAML works standalone — eject any time. There is no hosted product to be locked into.
